Penetration Testing FAQs

Penetration testing is a simulated cyberattack on a computer system, network, or web application to identify security vulnerabilities that could be exploited by malicious hackers.

Penetration testing helps organizations proactively identify and mitigate security weaknesses before attackers can exploit them. It allows for better security posture and helps in compliance with industry regulations.

Penetration tests are typically conducted by cybersecurity professionals with expertise in ethical hacking and penetration testing tools. They may be internal security teams or external consultants hired for the purpose.

The frequency depends on the organization's risk profile and industry regulations. It's typically recommended annually or after significant changes to the IT infrastructure.

Popular tools include Metasploit, Nmap, Burp Suite, OWASP ZAP, Nessus, and many more specialized tools depending on the type of testing.
